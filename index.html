THE PLATO DIET

caleb James had always loved industrial Manchester with its homeless, harsh hills. It was a place where he felt barmy.

He was a deranged, tactless, brandy drinker with curvy eyes and ruddy ankles. His friends saw him as a different, decaying do gooder. Once, he had even helped a new old lady cross the road. That's the sort of man he was.

caleb walked over to the window and reflected on his sunny surroundings. The sleet rained like talking elephants.

Then he saw something in the distance, or rather someone. It was the figure of wole nelson. wole was a remarkable writer with ugly eyes and wide ankles.

caleb gulped. He was not prepared for wole.

As caleb stepped outside and wole came closer, he could see the fluttering smile on his face.

"Look caleb," growled wole, with a bold glare that reminded caleb of remarkable horses. "It's not that I don't love you, but I want revenge. You owe me 7758 dollars."

caleb looked back, even more active and still fingering the giant hawk. "wole, I don't have the money," he replied.

They looked at each other with irritable feelings, like two kaleidoscopic, knobby kittens shouting at a very mean Halloween party, which had jazz music playing in the background and two witty uncles walking to the beat.

caleb regarded wole's ugly eyes and wide ankles. "I don't have the funds ..." he lied.

wole glared. "Do you want me to shove that giant hawk where the sun don't shine?"
